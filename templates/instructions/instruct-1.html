Instructions

This is an experiment related to trust in online applications.
You will interact with a hypothetical search engine, called ACME, for 20 times.
In each trial, you will be initially presented with a text box containing a predetermined keyword. As soon as you click on the Search button you will see:
a) a figure on the left hand side representing the first page of the results to your query and
b) an advertisement on the right hand side that may be related to the current query, the past ones, or one that is completely irrelevant
c) two slide-bars on the page bottom.
After observing the search engine performance results and advertisement, please read the question above each slide-bar and provide your response by moving the cursors to the desired locations.
As soon as you leave the curser a number from 0 (far left) to 100 (far right) will appear. Those values represent your (current) trust / distrust level to the ACME search engine in terms of its accuracy and confidentiality. You can either change your answers or proceed to the next trial by clicking on the Next button.
Please spend a few seconds at the keyword shown. Even though this is not a memory-related experiment, remembering the past keywords will help you in associating advertisements with past queries.
After interacting with the ACME search engine, you will be asked to answer a number of questions related to your personality and preferences. These answers will be used for research purposes only. Furthermore, these answers will be used to group you together with other participants that have similar answers and calculate the bonus that will be awarded to you. More specifically, you will have the chance to win an extra bonus if your answers during the last (20th) interaction with the ACME search engine is the closest one to the mean of the answers in your group.
The next instruction pages will help you get familiarized with the search process.
