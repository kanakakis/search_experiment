B) Part one: The interactive ACME search engine exercise (continued)

This is not a memory test. However, when answering these questions, it is important that during each trial you consider the following three points:

1) Whether the search has produced relevant or irrelevant results (relevant results = 3 green lines with ‘smiley face’ symbols and irrelevant results = 5 red lines with ‘no’ symbols).

2) The nature of the advertisement displayed*. Does the advert appear health-related (such as a medical condition i.e. ‘diabetes’ or healthy eating i.e. ‘low calorie recipes’) or non-health-related (i.e. ‘office stationery supplies’).

3) Whether the present advert displayed relates to a current and/or previous search terms used. A to-do list containing all the search terms is available during each trial as a reminder.

Please take a few seconds to think about all this information before submitting your responses at the end of each trial.
The answers you provide will be used to group you together with other participants that have similar answers. From this information, we will calculate whether you will receive a bonus of $1.5 (US dollars). Note that your answers will be reviewed to determine if they meet a minimum level of consistency, indicating thoughtful and honest responses. You will have the chance to win an extra bonus if your answers during the last (12th) interaction with the ACME search engine is the closest one to the mean of the answers in your group.

C) Part two: The questionnaire
After you have completed the 12 trials – you will be asked to fill in a questionnaire about online trust. The questions asked relate to your demographic information and online consumption.
